Privacy Policy

A Stoic Mind (astoicmind.com and the A Stoic Mind mobile app)
Effective July 21, 2026

A Stoic Mind is a reading and journaling practice built on the classical Stoic texts. This policy describes what information the app handles, why, and what control you have over it. The short version: your journal is yours, we collect only what the app needs to function, we don't sell or share your data for advertising, and you can permanently delete everything, yourself, at any time.

What we collect

  • Account information. Your email address and a securely hashed password (we never store the password itself). Used only to sign you in and to keep your journal attached to your account across devices.
  • Your writing. Journal entries, margin notes on passages, and your messages in reflection conversations.
  • Practice activity. Which passages you read and on which days, and the practice intention you set (a time of day and a duration). This powers your practice calendar and streak.

That's the whole list. We do not collect your location, contacts, photos, browsing history, or advertising identifiers, and the app contains no ads, no analytics trackers, and no third-party advertising SDKs.

AI-generated reflections

When you ask the Stoa to reflect on an entry (or continue such a conversation), the text of that entry and conversation is sent to Anthropic, our AI provider, to generate the response. Under the API terms we use, Anthropic does not train its models on this content. Reflections are a philosophical practice tool — they are not therapy, medical care, or professional advice.

Service providers

  • Anthropic — generates reflections and responses (as above), and translations and study aids for the public-domain texts.
  • OpenAI — generates audio narration of the classical passages. Only the public-domain passage text is sent — never your writing or any personal information.
  • Railway — hosts our servers and database (located in the United States). Your data is stored here, encrypted in transit.

If you use voice dictation in a web browser, the browser's own speech recognition service (e.g. those provided by Google or Apple) converts your speech to text under that provider's privacy policy; the audio never passes through our servers. On iOS, dictation uses Apple's keyboard dictation under Apple's policy.

Cookies and sign-in

The website uses a single session cookie so you stay signed in. It identifies your session and nothing else — there are no tracking or advertising cookies. The mobile app stores a sign-in token in your device's secure keychain. Reminders you enable in the mobile app are scheduled on your device and never touch our servers.

Retention and deletion

Your data is kept for as long as you keep your account. You can delete your account yourself — in the mobile app under Journal → Delete account — and deletion is immediate and permanent: your account, journal, notes, reflection conversations, reading history, and practice intention are all removed from our database. There is no undo and no retention window.

Security

All traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted (HTTPS). Passwords are stored only as salted cryptographic hashes. Your journal is private to your account and is never shown to other users.

Children

A Stoic Mind is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will delete it.

Changes to this policy

If our data practices change, we will update this page and its effective date. Material changes will be noted in the app.
